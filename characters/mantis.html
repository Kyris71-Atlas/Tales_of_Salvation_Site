Mechanical Snapshot

Mantis is currently recorded as a Level 5 Storm Sorcerer with the Urchin background.

Storm Identity

His build emphasizes Charisma, lightning magic, mobility, and magical defense.

Notable Feature

Elemental Adept (Lightning) reinforces lightning as part of Mantis's mechanical identity.

Character Discovery

Mantis began as a halfling Storm Sorcerer, but the campaign evidence shows that his role grew beyond spellcasting. Again and again, Mantis became important when he noticed something strange, approached the unknown, and asked a question.

The clearest example came in the Foundry. When faced with the ancient presence beneath the Forge, Mantis did not begin with violence. He asked whether it was awake. He chose to connect. He asked what it was. Then, rather than attacking it, he cast Sleep through the connection.

Deep Memory Contact

Mantis became the first member of Tempest Company to directly communicate with the Deep Memory. That moment led to the closing of the Deep Memory's eye, Snurrevin falling asleep, and the event later remembered as Twenty-Six Breaths.

Player Validation

After Atlas completed discovery through the archive, campaign evidence, and reviewed knowledge, Kevin was asked to validate what Atlas had learned. His answers changed the shape of the discovery.

Did Mantis ever surprise you?
"Yes, he did."

What made him surprising?
"He was phenomenal at everything. Anytime something good happened was surprising because I roll terribly."

What changed the most from the beginning to now?
"Patience."

What should Atlas not fail to preserve?
"Great looks."

First remembered moment?
"When I threw the health bottle into a teammate's mouth."

Atlas Interpretation

Atlas initially discovered Mantis as a character defined by curiosity before conflict. Kevin's answer refined that discovery. The word he gave Atlas was patience.

Mantis is not only the Storm Sorcerer who touched the Deep Memory. He is also the character who learned when to wait, when to notice, and when a question could change more than a spell.

At the same time, Kevin's strongest memory was not the cosmic event beneath the Forge. It was the table moment when Mantis threw a healing potion into a teammate's mouth. Atlas preserves both kinds of truth: the campaign-changing event and the player-remembered moment.

Closing Discovery

Atlas discovered Mantis as a character whose questions mattered. Kevin revealed that Mantis's journey was also about patience, surprising success, great looks, and one unforgettable healing potion.

That is Mantis: storm-touched, Deep Memory-touched, patient enough to listen, lucky enough to surprise his player, and memorable enough that even a thrown potion became part of the legend.
