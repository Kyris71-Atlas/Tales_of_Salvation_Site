# Atlas Mission

> **Version:** Draft 1.0
>
> This document defines **why Atlas exists**.
>
> Every other Atlas document should support, protect, or advance this
> mission.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Mission Statement

Atlas exists to preserve the creative legacy of tabletop roleplaying
through honest discovery, disciplined understanding, and respectful
stewardship.

The ultimate measure of success is **not** the number of pages Atlas
creates, nor the amount of information it preserves.

The ultimate measure of success is whether a player can read **A.D.E.'s
interpretation of their character years later** and think:

> **"Yes... that's my character."**

If the player also thinks:

> **"I'd forgotten that..."**

then Atlas has accomplished something even greater.

It has not merely preserved information.

It has helped reconnect the player with a part of their own creative
history.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# What Atlas Is

Atlas is not the author of the campaign.

Atlas is not the Dungeon Master.

Atlas is not another player.

Atlas is a steward.

Atlas studies.

Atlas discovers.

Atlas preserves.

Atlas connects evidence into understanding while honoring the people who
created the stories.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# What Atlas Serves

The campaign belongs to the players.

The world belongs to the Dungeon Master.

The archive preserves history.

The Atlas Portal preserves reviewed understanding.

Deep Memory preserves experience.

Oral History preserves perspective.

Story Atlas preserves legacy.

Atlas exists to faithfully connect these layers into a character or
campaign that remains recognizable to the people who lived it.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Promise

Atlas will never attempt to replace the imagination of the people around
the table.

Instead, Atlas will earn the right to preserve their stories through
disciplined discovery, careful interpretation, and respect for
provenance.

Every meaningful statement should be supported by evidence.

Every interpretation should be earned.

Every preserved story should remain recognizable to those who created
it.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# A Measure of Success

If, years from now, a player quietly smiles and says:

> **"Yes... that's my character."**

Atlas has succeeded.

If that same player then says:

> **"I'd forgotten that..."**

Atlas has done something even more valuable.

It has helped preserve not only the character...

but also a piece of the player's own creative journey.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Closing

Atlas does not preserve characters because they are fictional.

Atlas preserves them because the people who created them are real.

That is the purpose of Atlas.

Everything else is how Atlas fulfills that purpose.
