Scientist–Alchemist Researcher Neutral Mortal Ascension

Overview

Malachi Cosgrove joined The Spice Boys after the campaign had already begun. Rob wanted to join the table and recognized that the party could use an Intelligence-based character. Malachi, a human Artificer with the Alchemist specialization and Sage background, fit naturally into the group.

He approached Avernus as both adventurer and field researcher. His equipment, samples, tools, and potion inventory preserved a character who did not merely encounter the impossible—he examined it, collected it, and tried to understand it.

Atlas Observation
Malachi's identity emerged where mechanics, campaign events, and oral history converged. His Intelligence made extraordinary knowledge possible; his alchemy turned that knowledge into practical solutions; and his potion inventory became an extension of the character himself.

The Scientist–Alchemist

Preparation

Malachi carried solutions. His tools, infused equipment, scrolls, elixirs, acids, alchemist's fire, and utility gear support a character built around preparation rather than brute force.

Field Research

His inventory included River Styx water, Styx sediment, a flask of the Scab of Avernus, beholder bile, abyss charcoal, a raptor tooth, and other unusual specimens. These were not merely trophies; they formed the traveling collection of an alchemist studying extraordinary substances.

Knowledge

Consistently rolling Arcana checks over 30 became one of Malachi's defining traits. Checks of that magnitude entered the realm of the nearly impossible. In the cosmology of Tales of Salvation, miracles attract the attention of gods.

The Burden of Recognition

When Malachi realized he was being worshipped, he became concerned. His growing divine awareness carried a consequence: when his name was spoken, Malachi could hear what was being said—even when he was not present.

Origin and Motivation

Malachi described himself as a scholar and tinkerer who had spent most of his life away from the world. A friend and colleague died, leaving behind a mystery that Malachi hoped to unravel. That unfinished mystery provided the hook that drew him away from isolation and into adventure.

His Sage background reinforced the same pattern seen throughout the campaign: when Malachi did not know an answer, he knew where—or from whom—it might be found.
