Terms and Conditions of Manifestation, Intercession, Service, Counsel, Refusal, Appearance, Nonappearance, and Other Matters Relating to Sir Kluth

By drawing Kluth's Blade, invoking the name of Sir Kluth, requesting aid, failing to request aid when aid is clearly warranted, standing courageously, retreating questionably, or otherwise participating in events observed by the aforesaid knight, the wielder acknowledges the following terms.

✦   STAND FIRM. FEAR NOT.   ✦

1. Definitions

“The Blade” means Kluth's Blade, including its hilt, edge, lion sigil, inscriptions, dim radiance, ceremonial significance, and any metaphysical doorway reasonably associated therewith.

“The Wielder” means the currently attuned non-evil bearer of the Blade, whether acting wisely, rashly, heroically, reluctantly, or in a manner requiring later discussion.

“The Knight” means Sir Kluth, lawful good celestial spirit, sworn defender of the innocent, voice of encouragement, critic of unnecessary retreat, and sole interpreter of his own oath.

“Manifestation” means any visible, audible, martial, spectral, telepathic, symbolic, partial, complete, timely, untimely, requested, unrequested, noticed, or insufficiently appreciated appearance of the Knight.

2. Ordinary Wielder-Initiated Manifestation

The Wielder may, by means presently understood as a bonus action, call upon the Knight to appear within five feet for a period not ordinarily exceeding one minute.

This privilege is commonly believed to function once following each long rest.

The phrase “commonly believed” shall not be construed as a representation, warranty, admission, limitation, confession, mechanical ceiling, celestial incapacity, metaphysical battery rating, or binding statement by the Knight.

3. The Blade Is the Door; the Will Is His

The Blade provides a means of invitation. It does not provide ownership, compulsion, imprisonment, command authority, custody, containment, leverage, or a button.

The Wielder may call.

The Knight may answer.

The Knight may also decline, delay, arrive differently than expected, provide only counsel, appear in silence, or conclude that the Wielder has mistaken desire for necessity.

4. Independent Knightly Appearance

Nothing in these terms shall prevent the Knight from manifesting under his own authority whenever, in his judgment, a knight is needed.

Such judgment may consider, without being limited to:

  • danger to those unable to defend themselves;
  • acts of courage not yet recognized by the person performing them;
  • fear that must be faced rather than obeyed;
  • lines that must be held;
  • retreats that preserve life;
  • retreats that preserve only dignity poorly understood;
  • evil that has mistaken silence for surrender;
  • the honor of the Company;
  • the honor of the Wielder;
  • the possibility that the Wielder's honor is, at present, in need of assistance.

The foregoing examples are illustrative, nonexclusive, nonbinding, subject to interpretation, and not intended to create a dependable activation checklist.

5. Refusal of Summons

The Knight may refuse to manifest when the requested appearance would serve vanity, cruelty, greed, intimidation of the harmless, avoidance of responsibility, theatrical self-importance, shopping, wagering, tavern disputes beneath the dignity of steel, or any purpose the Knight considers unworthy.

Refusal shall not necessarily indicate abandonment, displeasure, weakness, distance, exhaustion, or inability.

Refusal may instead indicate judgment.

Suggested notice of refusal: “Not for this, my liege.” Additional explanation is optional and may be delivered later, during a more inconvenient moment.

6. Courage, Innocence, and Other Terms Not Further Defined

For purposes of this covenant, the meanings of courage, innocence, honor, mercy, necessity, cowardice, loyalty, duty, righteous anger, shameful pride, worthy cause, and “someone must stand here” shall remain intentionally undefined.

Any attempt to define these terms with sufficient precision to guarantee manifestation shall be considered evidence that the attempt has misunderstood the terms.

The Knight reserves the right to distinguish between two outwardly identical situations based upon motive, consequence, character, timing, oath, context, unseen danger, remembered promise, or reasons not yet explained.

7. Duration, Form, and Degree of Presence

The Knight may appear in full martial form for up to one minute, including use of his Spectral Longsword and Knightly Intercession.

He may also appear for less than one minute, more symbolically than physically, only long enough to speak, only long enough to stand between danger and another, or only long enough to make the Wielder reconsider a decision already in motion.

The form selected shall depend upon what the moment requires, not upon what is most convenient to track.

8. Knightly Intercession

When a nearby creature is struck, the Knight may intercede.

Such intercession is not insurance, indemnification, immunity, guaranteed coverage, automatic disadvantage, or evidence that the protected creature was correct.

The Knight may save a fool without endorsing the foolishness.

9. Relationship of the Parties

Sir Kluth is not a servant, summoned asset, secondary player character, hireling, possession, spell effect, consumable resource, rechargeable feature, mascot, or property supplied with the Blade.

He serves by oath and choice.

The Wielder is not his owner.

The Wielder may, however, be recognized by the Knight as his liege.

Such recognition does not excuse dishonor. It makes dishonor more disappointing.

Practical Effect:
Sir Kluth manifests when Sir Kluth judges that a knight is needed.

10. Apparent Contradictions

Where any clause appears to contradict another clause, both clauses shall remain in effect until the Knight determines which one matters.

Where no clause appears to apply, the Knight may act according to his oath.

Where every clause appears to apply, the Knight may act according to his oath.

Where the Wielder believes he has discovered a loophole, the loophole shall be presented to the Knight for immediate closing.

11. Amendments

These terms may not be amended by margin note, clever argument, arcane experimentation, repeated testing, adversarial interpretation, wishful thinking, or the phrase “technically speaking.”

They may be amended through deeds.

The Knight shall be the sole judge of whether a deed constitutes an amendment.

12. Acceptance

No signature is required.

Acceptance is demonstrated by continuing to bear the Blade after learning that the Knight is not bound as tightly as previously assumed.

The Knight's continued presence shall be understood as his own acceptance.
